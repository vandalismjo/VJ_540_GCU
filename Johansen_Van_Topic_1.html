Data Dictionary:

CRIM - per capita crime rate by town

ZN - proportion of residential land zoned for lots over 25,000 sq.ft.

INDUS - proportion of non-retail business acres per town.

CHAS - Charles River dummy variable (1 if tract bounds river; 0 otherwise)

NOX - nitric oxides concentration (parts per 10 million)

RM - average number of rooms per dwelling

AGE - proportion of owner-occupied units built prior to 1940

DIS - weighted distances to five Boston employment centres

RAD - index of accessibility to radial highways

TAX - full-value property-tax rate per $10,000

PTRATIO - pupil-teacher ratio by town

B - proportion of blacks by town

LSTAT - Percent lower status of the population

MEDV - Median value of owner-occupied homes in $1000's

Model Summary/Report¶

Why did I choose this model type?¶

I chose this model because the data was all numeric, and the target variable can be easily determined and modeled by an Ordinary Least Squares model. As certain variables increase or decrease, they have influence on the target variable of home price. From the assigned book for this course, the author explains "the training examples follow a distribution similar to that of future patterns (unseen by the machine during training) which the machine will receive; the machine will be required to give estimated output values ˆy for these patterns. If the training experience consists of data that lies in a region of the state space, then that region must be fully representative of situations over which the trained machine will later be used" (Gopal, p.47).

What is the mathematical Formula?¶

For OLS model the mathematical formula follows this pattern:

Y=Xβ+μ, where μ∼N(0,Σ)

In this example we can input our results and we get the following:

Y = -0.642X1 + 5.095X2 - 1.358

(with X1 being the LSTAT variable and X2 being the RM variable)

What other methods could have been used for future iterations?¶

If I were to continue the analysis and try a new modeling method, I would test out a LASSO Regression. It is similar to an OLS method, however, it utilizes shrinkage. Shrinkage is where data values are shrunk towards a central point, like the mean. The lasso procedure encourages simple, sparse models (i.e. models with fewer parameters). This particular type of regression is well-suited for models showing high levels of muticollinearity or when you want to automate certain parts of model selection, like variable selection/parameter elimination (Brownlee, 2019).

What are the variables used and their relationships?¶

In this model I chose to use the variables Rooms per House and Percent Lower Status of Population. These two variables have the highest correlation with the price of the house, target variable, as seen in the correlation matrix above. From the plots we can see as the number of rooms increase the price of the house increases and as the percent of lower status population decreases the price of the house increases.

What is the estimated error?¶

The estimated error for the model - on the training set was 5.64 and the testing set was 5.14

What are the key factors and their influence?¶

There are several factors that could have been used in the model, and in a future iterations I could test models with different factors and even try a LASSO model. From the correlation matrix we can see which factors have the most influence on our target variable of house price. As we already established, RM and LSTAT are the most highly influencial factors, however; other factors that could have high influence are PTRATIO and INDUS (pupil-teacher ratio by town and proportion of non-retail business acres per town).
